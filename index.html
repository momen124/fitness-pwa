⚠️ Safety Warning & Guidance

You are currently eating very high-volume, low-calorie meals and feeling great (no hunger or tiredness).

It is normal and common to lose faster than 0.8 kg per week in the first 4–8 weeks (sometimes 1.0 – 1.5 kg/week) due to water weight and big deficit.

Warning: Losing more than 1.2 kg per week for many weeks in a row can risk losing muscle mass, which we want to protect for strength, free diving, and kitesurfing.

When to Increase Food

  • If your weekly average weight loss is consistently over 1.2 kg per week for 2–3 weeks AND you still feel good → Slightly increase calories.
  • How to increase: Add 1 extra egg at breakfast, bigger Greek yogurt, increase chicken breast to 200-250g at lunch, or add one whey protein shake as snack.
  • Goal: Bring weekly loss back to 0.7 – 1.0 kg per week.

How to Track Safely & Protect Muscle

  • Weigh yourself every morning after bathroom, before eating (same conditions).
  • Always track weekly average weight, not daily fluctuations.
  • Do InBody scan every 4 weeks under same conditions.
  • Watch for signs: sudden increase in hunger, tiredness, poor strength performance, or stalled weight loss → increase food immediately.
  • Keep strength training 3x per week — this helps protect muscle.
  • Protein must stay high (150-180g) even when increasing calories.
  • Prioritize: Walk/Cycle + Protein + Strength > Perfect calorie counting.
